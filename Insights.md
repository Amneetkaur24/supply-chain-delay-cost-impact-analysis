# Business Insights

This analysis explored shipment delays in an automotive supply chain and identified operational patterns influencing delivery performance and financial impact.

---

# Shipment Delay Rate

62% of car shipments experienced delays.

This high delay rate suggests systemic inefficiencies across the supply chain, particularly in supplier reliability and component delivery timing.

---

# Critical Component Risk

Delays in critical components strongly influence full vehicle shipment delays.

The engine and brake systems are the most influential components, with engine reliability showing the strongest relationship with financial loss.

---

# Financial Impact of Delays

Financial losses increase significantly as delay days increase.

There is a strong positive relationship between delay duration and financial impact, indicating that even small operational improvements in delivery times could reduce costs substantially.

---

# Seasonal Patterns

Shipment delays were highest during Q1 (January–March).

Possible causes include:

• supplier backlog  
• post-holiday supply disruptions  
• operational ramp-up after the new year

---

# Supplier Reliability

Supplier reliability scores vary significantly across components.

Transmission and suspension suppliers show lower reliability scores compared to other parts, suggesting opportunities for supplier performance improvement.

---

# Urgent Orders

High urgency orders experience slightly higher delay rates.

This indicates potential planning or scheduling inefficiencies where urgent shipments are not being prioritized effectively.

---

# Inventory Buffer Usage

Inventory buffer usage appears limited and does not significantly reduce delay probability.

This suggests that buffer stock strategies may not be optimally implemented.

---

# Production Shift Impact

Evening production shifts show slightly higher delay frequency and financial impact.

This may indicate workforce or operational efficiency differences between shifts.

---

# Operational Recommendations

Based on the analysis, companies should consider:

• Improving supplier reliability monitoring  
• Increasing buffer inventory for critical components  
• Enhancing logistics planning during Q1 periods  
• Monitoring evening shift operational performance  
• Prioritizing urgent shipments more effectively
